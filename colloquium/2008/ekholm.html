KTH/SU Mathematics Colloquium

08-02-27

Tobias Ekholm, Uppsala University

Holomorphic curves and Morse type inequalities for exact Lagrangian immersions

A series of conjectures in symplectic geometry (formulated by Arnold) generalizes the Morse inequalities from finite dimensional topology to problems in symplectic and contact geometry. In groundbreaking works from the 1980's Gromov and Floer used holomorphic curves to establish some of these conjectures. In the talk we discuss how to apply holomorphic curve techniques to demonstrate important special cases of the following (still open) conjecture: the number of double points of any exact Lagrangian submanifold of complex n-space is at least as large as half the rank of its total homology.
